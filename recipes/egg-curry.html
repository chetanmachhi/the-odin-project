Egg Curry

Description

Egg curry is a popular Indian dish where boiled eggs are cooked in a spiced, flavorful gravy. The gravy is typically made from a base of onions, tomatoes, and a variety of spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The eggs are usually added to the curry towards the end of cooking, allowing them to absorb the flavors of the sauce. It’s often enjoyed with rice, naan, or roti.

Ingredients

Here's a detailed recipe for egg curry, with all the ingredients listed in precise measurements:

Vegetables

  • Onions: 2 medium-sized (about 200 grams), finely chopped
  • Tomatoes: 2 medium-sized (about 200 grams), finely chopped
  • Green chilies: 2, slit lengthwise
  • Garlic: 4 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece (about 10 grams), minced
  • Coriander leaves: A handful (about 15 grams), chopped (for garnishing)

Spices

  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon (about 2 grams)
  • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon (about 4 grams)
  • Coriander powder: 1 tablespoon (about 8 grams)
  • Garam masala: 1 tablespoon (about 10 grams)
  • Cumin seeds: 1 teaspoon (about 4 grams)
  • Bay leaves: 2

Main & Additional Ingredients

  • Eggs: 6, boiled and peeled
  • Water: 4 cups (1 liter)
  • Oil: 3 tablespoons (about 45 milliliters)
  • Salt: To taste (about 1 teaspoon)
  • Yogurt: 1/2 cup (120 milliliters)
  • Butter: 1 tablespoon (about 15 grams)

Steps

  1. Boil 6 eggs, peel them, and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Base:
    • Heat 3 tablespoons of oil in a pan.
    • Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and 2 bay leaves, and let them splutter.
    • Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add minced garlic and ginger, and sauté for another 2 minutes.
    • Add the green chilies.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil separates from the mixture.
  4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook the mixture until the spices are well blended with the onion-tomato base.
  5. Add 1/2 cup of yogurt and mix well. Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add 4 cups of water and bring the curry to a boil. Make small slits on the boiled eggs and add them to the curry.
  7. Reduce the heat and let the curry simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  8. Add 1 tablespoon of garam masala and 1 tablespoon of butter. Stir well.
  9. Finish with Garam Masala and Butter
  10. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or chapati.

Enjoy your egg curry!
